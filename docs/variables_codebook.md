# Variables Codebook

This document details the definition, raw sources, construction steps, and era-comparability of the primary variables and indices in the *Pendulum Always Swings* project.

---

## 1. Policy Intensity (`policy_intensity`)

*   **Concept**: The strength and high-stakes nature of state-level education accountability mandates.
*   **Raw Source**: Education Commission of the States (ECS) Legislative Archives, State Department of Education policy documents, and consolidated state ESSA plans.
*   **Years Covered**: 2010–2024.
*   **Components**: A sum of four time-varying indicators (range: $[0, 4]$):
    1.  `exit_exam` (1/0): Presence of a high school standardized graduation exam.
    2.  `af_grading` (1/0): Presence of a letter-grade (A–F) school ranking system.
    3.  `third_grade_retention` (1/0): Presence of a high-stakes reading test retention law.
    4.  `vam_eval` (1/0): Presence of value-added model (VAM) test scores linked to teacher evaluations.
*   **Standardization**: Standardized within-era (pre-ESSA: $\le 2017$, post-ESSA: $\ge 2018$) to control for the nationwide shift in accountability regimes.
*   **Comparability**: Within-era standardization preserves state rankings while adjusting for the structural shift in mean pressure across eras.

---

## 2. Bounded Norm (`norm`)

*   **Concept**: The public's adaptive expectation of policy intensity.
*   **Mathematical Formula**:
    $$N_{s,t} = \nu P_{s,t-1} + (1 - \nu) N_{s,t-1}$$
    with learning rate $\nu = 0.08$.
*   **Raw Source**: Calculated from the lag of `policy_intensity` starting in 2010.
*   **Interpretation**: An exponentially weighted moving average (EWMA) of past policy intensity, representing how societal expectations adapt to policy over time.

---

## 3. Backlash Index (`backlash`)

*   **Concept**: Organized public and elite resistance to high-stakes education policies.
*   **Raw Sources**:
    -   *Media Attention*: GDELT Project (standardized media mentions of education policy themes).
    -   *Mass Search Interest*: Google Trends Relative Search Volume (RSV) for "Common Core," "opt out testing," and "standardized testing."
*   **Years Covered**: 2010–2024.
*   **CFA Results & Fallback**:
    -   Pre-registered Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA) using `semopy` loaded onto a single latent factor yielded a poor fit: $\text{CFI} = 0.040$, $\text{RMSEA} = 0.368$.
    -   Because the CFA fit failed to meet the validation threshold ($\text{CFI} > 0.95, \text{RMSEA} < 0.06$), we fell back to the **Principal Component Analysis (PCA) first component** of the state-demeaned indicators.
    -   The first principal component (`backlash_pca`) explains **44.58%** of the total variance and serves as the primary backlash composite.

---

## 4. Disaggregated Policy Indices (`policy_labor` and `policy_community`)

*   **Concept**: Accountability pressure targeted at teachers vs. parents/students.
*   **Disaggregation Rules**:
    -   `raw_labor`: Teacher-salient stakes, operationalized as `vam_eval`.
    -   `raw_comm`: Parent/student-salient stakes, operationalized as `exit_exam + af_grading + third_grade_retention`.
*   **Post-ESSA Plan Weighting**:
    -   Post-2017, these raw indices are scaled by the weights chosen in each state's consolidated ESSA plan:
        $$\text{RawComm}_{s,t} = \text{RawComm}_{s,t} \times \left(\frac{\text{AcademicAchievementWeight}_s}{100}\right)$$
        $$\text{RawLabor}_{s,t} = \text{RawLabor}_{s,t} \times \left(\frac{\text{AcademicGrowthWeight}_s}{100}\right)$$
    -   These weighted raw indices are then standardized within-era.

---

## 5. ESEA Waiver Status (`has_waiver`)

*   **Concept**: Active federal waiver granting flexibility from NCLB provisions.
*   **Raw Source**: U.S. Department of Education ESEA Flexibility Archives.
*   **Years Covered**: 2012–2017 (waiver programs expired with the implementation of ESSA).
*   **Coding Details**: Coding is 1 during active waiver spells. Documented waiver revocations are explicitly coded:
    -   *Washington State*: Revoked in 2014 (coded as 0 for 2014–2017) due to refusal to mandate test-linked teacher evaluations.
    -   *Oklahoma*: Revoked in 2014 (coded as 0 for 2014).

---

## 6. Placebo Policy (`placebo_policy_intensity` and `highway_backlash`)

*   **Concept**: Falsification control variables to test domain-specificity.
*   **Permuted Placebo**:
    -   `placebo_policy_intensity`: Generated by randomly permuting state policy intensity histories across states. This breaks the link between a state's policy and its backlash while preserving autocorrelation and distribution properties.
*   **Highway Placebo**:
    -   `highway_policy_intensity`: Standardized federal highway obligation funding apportionments (FHWA).
    -   `highway_backlash`: Standardized real Google Trends search interest (`svi_highway`) and GDELT media salience (`gdelt_highway_salience`) for highway funding.
